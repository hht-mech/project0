Graduate

YTU offers a wide range of postgraduate programmes catering for a variety of interests, namely: Doctoral degree programmes take minimum three years after relevant Master’s degrees. In the first year, the students have to attend the coursework, at which the essential subjects for each programme are to be studied. And they will have another two years for their theses. Master’s degree programmes are primarily designed to do coursework and thesis. The programmes take minimum two years. The first year is for coursework and the second year is for thesis. For those, who want to continue the study (to attend Ph. D.) must be excellent in both coursework and thesis. Postgraduate Diploma programmes provide specialized training in specific field of study. Postgraduate Diploma is often favoured by students who seek additional exposure to an area of interest without committing themselves to longer-term further studies.

Requirements for Graduate

Specialization begins from the First Year of the studies. Duration for the first degree is four years (Law degree is five years). There are two semesters in each academic year (December – March and June – September) examinations are held at the end of each semester. A student is expected to complete a specific class at most within two consecutive academic years. Otherwise, he or she will be permitted to repeat the same examination once more as an external student only in the following academic year. Fourth year students are required to sit for both written and viva voce examinations. In addition, they will be required to submit a term-paper. Students are required to obtain a minimum of Grade Point 3 in each subject. The grading system of undergraduate degree courses and Honours degree courses is as follows. The duration of degree programme is as follows:

  • First Class Honours
  • Second Class Honours (First Division)
  • Second Class Honours (Second Division)
  • Third Class Honours
  • B.A/B.Sc (Ordinary degree)

ABOUT YTU

The present Yangon Technological University (Y.T.U) began as the Department of Engineering in 1923 under the Rangoon (Yangon) University. The Engineering Department was situated downtown, on the Bogyoke Aung San street. B.Sc. (Engineering) program in Civil Engineering began in 1924.
